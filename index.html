The problem

Most B2B teams do not have a content problem. They have a context problem.

Your expertise is scattered across your website, LinkedIn profile, articles, sales calls, decks, case studies, proposals, and founder thinking. Buyers need clarity. AI systems need usable source material.

Buyers cannot repeat the value.

They understand you only after a call because the public authority system does not explain the problem, proof, offer, and difference clearly enough.

AI systems cannot explain you accurately.

If your source layer is thin, vague, or scattered, AI answers become generic, incomplete, or competitor-shaped.

Your best proof is buried.

The material that should earn trust lives in calls, decks, testimonials, founder notes, and internal docs instead of public authority assets.

What gets audited

The AI Search Authority Scorecard

Each area is scored from 0 to 5, then translated into a practical authority roadmap: what is clear, what is confusing, what is missing, and what should be built next.

1. Positioning clarity

Can a buyer or AI system explain what you do in one clean sentence?

2. Category/entity clarity

Are you associated with the right category, problems, buyers, and alternatives?

3. Source material depth

Do you have enough structured, crawlable material for buyers and AI systems to learn from?

4. Proof architecture

Are your strongest claims supported by visible proof, examples, outcomes, methods, and evidence?

5. Buyer-language alignment

Do your pages use the language prospects use when they describe the problem?

6. Offer clarity

Is the first commercial step obvious, specific, and easy to buy?

7. Website authority structure

Do your homepage, service pages, proof pages, and articles form a connected source layer?

8. LinkedIn trust surface

Does your profile, featured section, posts, newsletter, and activity support the authority story?

9. Newsletter/content memory

Does your publishing create repeated market memory or just isolated smart posts?

10. AI answer quality

What do AI tools say when asked about you, your category, your alternatives, and your credibility?

11. Authority asset coverage

Which assets are missing: proof page, comparison page, source page, report, founder POV, lead magnet, or authority hub?

12. Conversion path clarity

Can attention move into a diagnostic, sales conversation, sprint, or implementation path without friction?

FAQ

Plain answers before the call.

Is this a GEO or SEO audit?

It includes AI search and SEO-adjacent signals, but it is not a traditional keyword or technical SEO audit. The focus is authority readiness: source material, proof, buyer language, trust surfaces, and whether AI systems can understand and cite the company accurately.

Do you guarantee AI search rankings?

No. The audit does not promise guaranteed rankings or citations. It identifies what makes your authority system easier or harder for buyers and AI systems to understand, verify, cite, trust, and remember.

How much do clients need to upload?

Enough to show the real authority system: website links, LinkedIn profiles, service pages, case studies, sales decks, testimonials, lead magnets, reports, newsletters, and any internal positioning or sales material worth reviewing.

What happens after the audit?

The audit recommends the next highest-leverage asset or system fix. That may be a proof page, source page, authority asset, LinkedIn trust surface rebuild, newsletter system, service-page rewrite, or broader authority operations sprint.
